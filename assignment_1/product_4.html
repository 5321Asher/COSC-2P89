Mercedes-Benz Sl65 AMG Black Series

The Mercedes-Benz SL65 AMG Black Series stands as one of AMG’s most extreme creations — a raw, unapologetic powerhouse born from the pursuit of pure performance. Built on the R230 chassis, the Black Series takes the luxury grand tourer DNA of the SL-Class and reimagines it as a race-bred monster for the road. Its wide stance, aggressive aero, and menacing presence signal its intent long before the engine roars to life. The SL65 Black Series isn’t just a car — it’s a statement of excess, engineering, and precision, capturing the height of AMG’s golden era.

Price: $450,000

Engine and Performance

At its core lies a handcrafted 6.0-liter twin-turbocharged V12 engine delivering an immense 661 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. Power is sent to the rear wheels through a 5-speed AMG Speedshift automatic transmission — one of the few capable of handling such monstrous torque. The Black Series sprints from 0 to 100 km/h in just 3.8 seconds and continues on to an electronically limited top speed of 320 km/h. AMG reworked nearly every component of the standard SL65, adding larger turbos, improved cooling, and a lighter titanium exhaust system. The result is an experience that’s both brutal and smooth — a combination of overwhelming thrust and turbine-like refinement that defines AMG’s V12 mastery.

Design & Interior

The design of the SL65 AMG Black Series is all about functional aggression. Its widened carbon-fiber body, fixed rear wing, and vented fenders serve both aerodynamic and aesthetic purposes, giving it a stance more akin to a DTM race car than a roadster. Inside, the cabin strikes a balance between stripped-down performance and high-end craftsmanship. Carbon-fiber trim replaces traditional wood or aluminum, while lightweight bucket seats and Alcantara accents keep the focus on driving. The retractable hardtop of the standard SL has been removed and replaced with a fixed carbon roof to save weight — a clear signal that this isn’t a cruiser, it’s a precision tool.

Driving Dynamics

Despite its intimidating power, the SL65 AMG Black Series is remarkably composed once in motion. The adjustable coilover suspension and wider track give it immense stability, while the carbon-ceramic brakes provide immense stopping confidence. The steering is weighty and deliberate, communicating the car’s mass and power honestly through every input. Push it hard, and the V12 surges with seemingly endless torque, accompanied by a deep, thunderous exhaust note that feels more aircraft than automobile. It’s not a car that flatters mistakes — it demands respect — but when driven with precision, it delivers one of the most thrilling and visceral experiences ever to come out of Affalterbach.
